  - heading: Common questions
    items:
      - question: How does it work?
        answer: Our tracker visits the websites of major Australian golf retailers every night and records the price of every club they list. When two stores sell the same club, we match them up so you can compare like for like. The difference between the dearest and cheapest store — the spread — is often more than $200 on a current-model driver.
      - question: Are the prices always accurate?
        answer: Prices are as fresh as our last nightly check, but stores can change a price at any moment and stock can sell out. Always confirm the price on the retailer's own site before you buy — every price on LowballGolf links straight to the store so you can check in one click.
      - question: Which stores do you track?
        answer: We currently track Drummond Golf, Power Golf, Golf Paradise, The Golf Factory and House of Golf, with more Australian retailers on the way. If there's a store you'd like to see included, tell us via the contact page.
      - question: How does LowballGolf make money?
        answer: When affiliate partnerships are in place, we may earn a small commission if you buy through a link on this site. It never affects the price you pay, and it never affects which store we show as cheapest — the numbers decide that, not us.

LowballGolf started with a simple observation: the exact same driver can cost hundreds of dollars more at one Australian golf shop than another, and nobody has time to check five websites every day. So we built a tracker that does it for us — and for you.

If something looks wrong, or you want a club or store added, [get in touch](/contact).
